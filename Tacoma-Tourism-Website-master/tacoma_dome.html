Tacoma Dome

Overview

The Tacoma Dome is one of the largest wood domed structures in the world. It is owned and operated by the City of Tacomaâ€™s Venues & Events Department. Flexibility is the key to the success of the Tacoma Dome. With new state of the art telescopic seating and an extensive curtaining system, almost any event is possible. The unique configurations mean the Tacoma Dome can accommodate 1,000 to 21,000 patrons, making it the largest indoor venue in the state of Washington.

History

On July 1, 1981 the Tacoma Dome started construction with the aspirations to be a "facility that has the greatest flexibility of any structure of its kind in the world". It took 19.5 months to complete construction of the 152 foot tall and 530 foot staple of the Tacoma area. It was a massive effort that took over 320,000 nails and 260,000 square feet of concrete to form such a enourmous yet sturdy structure that has come to be one of the defining landmarks of the city. Events

The Tacoma Dome hosts a plethora of events such as indoor sporting events, concerts, festivals, and more. The enormous size of the dome and it's free-form and easy to transform nature makes it the largest event center in the Pacific Northwest, and has made it the main event hosting center in the state. This also means that there are constantly events being held every week and help accomodate tourists with exciting experiences. These events are clearly listed on the Tacoma Dome's event page and shouldn't be overlooked!
